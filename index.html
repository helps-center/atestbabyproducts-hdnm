**1. Baby Bottles**

*   **Types**
    *   *Material:*
        *   Plastic: These are light and last a while. Just be sure they say BPA-free.
        *   Glass: No chemicals, easy to sterilize, but they can be heavy and break if you drop them.
        *   Silicone: Soft, bendy, and no weird chemicals. They don't break as easily as glass.
    *   *Nipple Types*
        *   Flow: Get slow ones for newborns, then move up to medium and fast as they get older.
        *   Shape: They come in standard, orthodontic (which are supposed to be good for their teeth), and wide-neck.
        *   Material: Silicone is common because it lasts and is easy to clean. Latex is softer, but some babies are allergic.
    *   *Cool Features:*
        *   Anti-Colic: Some have special valves to help prevent gas.
        *   Self-Sterilizing: A few can be sterilized in the microwave or by boiling.
        *   Formula Ready: A couple let you add the formula right into the bottle.

*   **Using Them and Keeping Them Clean:**

    *   Wash everything before you use it the first time, and after every feeding.
    *   Sterilize bottles until your baby is at least 6 months old.
    *   If the nipples are cracked or look weird, replace them.
    *   Don't microwave bottles with milk or formula inside – it can get too hot in some spots.

**2. Strollers**

*   **Types**
    *   Standard: Good for everyday stuff. They usually have a basket underneath and the handle adjusts.
    *   Lightweight/Frame Strollers: Super easy to fold up and bring with you. Great for trips.
    *   Travel Systems: These come with a car seat that clicks right into the stroller.
    *   Jogging Strollers: Big wheels, good suspension, and a handbrake.
    *   Double Strollers: For twins or when you have two little ones. The seats can be side-by-side or one in front of the other.

*   **Safety Stuff**
    *   Make sure it has a 5-point harness (that goes over the shoulders and around the waist).
    *   The brakes should lock the back wheels, and sometimes the front ones too.
    *   If the front wheels swivel, make sure they can lock in place.
    *   Reflective stuff is good to have so cars can see you.
*   **Taking Care of It:**
    *   Check the wheels and brakes regularly.
    *   Clean the fabric so it doesn't get moldy.
    *   Fold it up right so it doesn't break

**3. Baby Monitors**

*   **Types**
    *   Audio: Just lets you hear the baby. Some have volume controls.
    *   Video: You can see the baby on a screen or your phone. Some have night vision and move around.
    *   Wi-Fi: Connects to your phone, sends you alerts. A few can even tell you the temperature in the room or track breathing.
    *   Movement: You attach a sensor to the crib or baby to see if they're moving or breathing. It alerts you if they stop.
*   **Safety**
    *   Keep the monitor away from cords.
    *   Test how far away it works and how long the battery lasts.
    *   If it's a Wi-Fi monitor, make sure the connection is secure.

**4. Cribs, Bassinets, and Sleep Stuff**

*   *Crib Safety:*
    *   The slats should be close together (no more than 2.4 inches apart).
    *   Use a firm mattress.
    *   Don't put soft blankets or pillows in there.
*   *Bassinets:* Good for newborns, but make sure they're safe.
*   *Mattress:* Firm and fits snugly in the crib.
*   *Sleep Positioners:* Most experts say not to use these. Babies should sleep on their backs on a firm surface.

**5. Feeding Stuff**

*   *Breastfeeding:* Breast pump, milk bags, and a nursing pillow can help.
*   *Formula:* You'll need measuring spoons, a sterilizer, and maybe a bottle warmer.
*   *When They Start Eating Solids:* Soft spoons, bowls, and sippy cups.

**6. Diapering**

*   *Diapers:* Cloth or disposable, whatever you like.
*   *Wipes:* Get the unscented kind for newborns.
*   *Changing Pads:* Waterproof and easy to wipe down.
*   *Other Stuff:* Diaper rash cream, nail clippers, and baby soap/shampoo.

**7. Travel Gear**

*   *Car Seats:* Rear-facing for babies. Then convertible or booster seats as they grow.
*   *Baby Carriers:* Soft ones, structured ones, or wraps.
*   *Diaper Bags:* Look for lots of pockets and a place to keep bottles cold.

**8. Safety Stuff**

*   Outlet covers, corner guards, and cabinet locks.
*   Thermometers for forehead, ear, or just a regular digital one.
*   A baby first aid kit.

**Some Tips:**

*   Always check for recalls.
*   Think about how well it works and how safe it is before you worry about how it looks.
*   Clean everything often and check it for damage.
*   Introduce new stuff slowly so your baby can get used to it.
